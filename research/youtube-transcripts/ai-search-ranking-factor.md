# AI Search Ranking Factor - Brand Mentions & Retrieval Sources

## Video Link
https://youtu.be/mB0QuXEv_sg?si=B9XKWJyopKY-N7rB

## Summary
This content explains that ranking in AI search is not driven solely by traditional SEO. Instead, the most important factor is how frequently a brand appears across key third-party sources (retrieval sources) that AI systems use to generate answers.

---

## Extracted Content (Structured Summary)

### 🔹 AI Search vs Traditional SEO
- Traditional SEO alone is not sufficient for AI visibility  
- AI search results depend on multiple data sources, not just rankings  
- A brand can rank well in search but still lack AI visibility  

👉 Insight: AI search introduces a new layer beyond traditional SEO

---

### 🔹 Core Ranking Factor: Brand Mentions on Retrieval Sources
- AI platforms pull data from multiple external sources  
- Brands that appear frequently across these sources dominate AI answers  
- Example: A brand may not be cited directly but still influences results  

👉 Key Finding:
- High brand presence across sources = higher AI visibility  

---

### 🔹 Case Insight (HubSpot Example)
- HubSpot appears in **89% of citation sources**  
- Despite not being directly cited, it is heavily mentioned  
- This leads to strong AI visibility  

👉 Insight: Mentions matter more than direct citations

---

### 🔹 Links Are Not Required
- 72% of mentions are **unlinked**  
- Even nofollow or sponsored links still contribute  
- AI does not differentiate between link types  

👉 Key Takeaway:
- Brand mentions > backlinks (in AI context)

---

### 🔹 Strategy: Citation-Based SEO (New Approach)

#### Step 1: Identify Commercial Keywords
- Focus on queries like “best CRM”  
- Avoid informational queries  

#### Step 2: Extract AI Citations
- Use platforms like:
  - ChatGPT  
  - Google AI Overviews  
  - Gemini  
  - Perplexity  
  - Claude  

#### Step 3: Build Citation Database
- Collect all sources into a spreadsheet  
- Identify frequently recurring domains  

#### Step 4: Prioritize High-Frequency Sources
- Sources appearing across multiple platforms = highest priority  

---

### 🔹 Execution Strategy (Practical Approach)

- Reach out to citation sources  
- Offer:
  - Sponsored content  
  - Paid placements  
  - Collaborations  

👉 Simple outreach message:
“Do you accept sponsored posts? I’m interested in advertising.”

---

### 🔹 Earned Media vs Owned Media

#### High Competition Niches
- Earned media (third-party mentions) is critical  
- Owned assets (website, social) have limited influence  

#### Medium Competition
- Mix of owned + earned media  

#### Low Competition (Local SEO)
- Owned assets become more effective  
- Businesses themselves can be retrieval sources  

👉 Insight: Strategy depends on competition level

---

### 🔹 Importance of Off-Site SEO
- External signals build trust for AI systems  
- Weak backlink/profile = poor performance  
- Off-site work should begin from day one  

👉 Key Principle:
- Off-site authority drives both SEO and AI visibility  

---

### 🔹 Traditional Link Building Still Matters
- Helps rank in traditional search  
- Enables your site to become a retrieval source  

BUT:
- Must be combined with **brand mention strategy**

---

### 🔹 Key Execution Framework

1. Identify commercial keywords  
2. Extract AI citations across platforms  
3. Build a prioritized list  
4. Focus on high-frequency sources  
5. Secure brand mentions (paid or organic)  

---

## Key Insights
- Brand mentions are the primary driver of AI search visibility  
- AI systems rely on multiple external sources, not just rankings  
- Unlinked mentions are highly influential  
- Citation extraction is a powerful SEO strategy  
- Earned media is critical in competitive industries  

---

## Observations
This content represents a shift from traditional SEO to **AI-driven visibility optimization**. It highlights that influence across trusted sources matters more than direct rankings, making brand presence and authority the key success factors.
